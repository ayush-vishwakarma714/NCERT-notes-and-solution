Chapter 1: Matter in Our Surroundings

Introduction

Everything around us occupies space and has mass. Such things are called matter. In this chapter, we study the physical nature of matter, its states, and how it changes due to temperature and pressure.

1. What is Matter?

  • Matter is anything that has mass and occupies space.
  • Examples: air, water, stone, milk.

2. Characteristics of Particles of Matter

  • Particles of matter are very small.
  • They have spaces between them.
  • They are constantly moving.
  • They attract each other.

Question 1

Define matter. Give two examples.

Answer:

Matter is anything that has mass and occupies space. Examples of matter include air and water.

Question 2

List any three characteristics of particles of matter.

Answer:

  • Particles of matter are very small.
  • They have spaces between them.
  • They are continuously moving.

Question 3

Why can gases be compressed easily?

Answer:

Gases can be compressed easily because the particles of gases have large spaces between them.
